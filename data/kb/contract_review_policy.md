# Contract Review Policy

## Payment Terms - Standard Position

Preferred payment terms are Net 30 or Net 45 after receipt of a valid invoice.
Invoices should be billed in arrears unless a signed order form expressly
approves prepayment. Payment obligations should be conditioned on receipt of a
valid invoice, tax documentation, and purchase order details where required.

## Payment Terms - Procurement Review Triggers

Net 60, annual prepayment, milestone billing without acceptance criteria,
auto-charge provisions, late fees above 1.5% per month, and payment due on
receipt require procurement review. Prepayment above 25% of annual fees also
requires procurement review unless the vendor is already approved for that
commercial model.

## Payment Terms - Prohibited Positions

The company should not accept payment terms that require payment before contract
signature, waive invoice dispute rights, impose unilateral vendor price changes
inside a committed term, or allow service suspension while a good-faith invoice
dispute is pending. These terms require escalation before signature.

## Liability - Standard Cap

Standard vendor contracts should cap aggregate liability at the greater of fees
paid or payable in the previous twelve months. Mutual liability caps are
preferred. Liability exclusions should cover indirect, incidental, special,
consequential, exemplary, and punitive damages.

## Liability - Carveouts

Acceptable liability carveouts include confidentiality breaches, data security
breaches caused by the vendor, gross negligence, willful misconduct,
infringement indemnity, and payment obligations. Carveouts should be mutual
where the risk applies to both parties.

## Liability - Legal Review Triggers

Unlimited liability, uncapped indemnity, customer-only liability exposure,
consequential damages exposure, exclusion of vendor confidentiality liability,
or vendor liability capped below six months of fees requires legal review.
Any clause making the customer responsible for third-party claims caused by the
vendor also requires legal review.

## Data Usage - Standard Position

Vendors may process customer data only to provide, secure, support, and improve
the contracted services for the customer. Aggregated analytics are acceptable
only when they cannot identify the company, users, customers, or confidential
business information.

## Data Usage - AI Restrictions

Vendor use of company data, customer data, personal data, prompts, outputs, or
confidential information to train, fine-tune, evaluate, or improve AI models is
prohibited unless legal approves explicit written language. AI subprocessors,
model hosting, prompt logging, and human review of prompts or outputs must be
disclosed before approval.

## Data Usage - Privacy and Security Requirements

Contracts involving personal data must include a data processing agreement,
subprocessor disclosure, security controls, breach notice, data deletion rights,
and audit or assurance evidence such as SOC 2 Type II, ISO 27001, or equivalent
documentation. Missing privacy terms require legal review.

## Termination - Standard Position

Contracts should include termination for material breach if the breach is not
cured within 30 days after written notice. For SaaS, hosted tools, or vendors
handling customer data, termination for convenience on 30 days notice is
preferred.

## Termination - Legal Review Triggers

Vendor-only termination rights, immediate suspension without notice, termination
fees, survival of broad payment obligations after termination, or loss of data
export rights requires legal review. Any clause allowing the vendor to retain
customer data after termination must be reviewed.

## Renewal - Standard Position

Automatic renewal is acceptable only when non-renewal notice is 60 days or less
and renewal pricing is capped or clearly disclosed. Renewal terms should preserve
existing legal protections, data processing terms, and security obligations.

## Renewal - Procurement Review Triggers

Renewal notice periods above 60 days, uncapped renewal price increases, evergreen
terms, renewal pricing tied only to vendor list price, or automatic renewal
after a trial requires procurement review.

## Governing Law - Preferred Jurisdictions

Company-preferred governing law is Delaware, New York, California, England and
Wales, or the company's home jurisdiction. Neutral commercial arbitration may be
acceptable for international vendors if injunctive relief remains available for
confidentiality and intellectual property breaches.

## Governing Law - Legal Review Triggers

Foreign governing law outside preferred jurisdictions, exclusive venue in a
vendor-friendly forum, waiver of injunctive relief, class action waiver tied to
consumer terms, or mandatory arbitration that limits emergency remedies requires
legal review.
