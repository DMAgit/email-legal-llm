# Clause Library

## Acceptable Liability Clause

Each party's aggregate liability under this agreement is limited to the fees
paid or payable in the twelve months before the event giving rise to the claim.
Neither party is liable for indirect, incidental, special, consequential, or
punitive damages, except for confidentiality breaches, data security breaches,
infringement indemnity, willful misconduct, and payment obligations.

## Negotiable Liability Clause

Vendor's aggregate liability is limited to fees paid in the six months before
the claim. Confidentiality breaches and infringement indemnity are excluded from
the cap, but data security breaches are not listed as a carveout. This clause is
negotiable and should be routed to legal if customer data is processed.

## Prohibited Liability Clause

Customer is liable for all direct, indirect, consequential, incidental, special,
exemplary, and punitive damages without limitation, including claims caused by
vendor systems or vendor personnel. Vendor's total liability is capped at fees
paid in the previous month. This clause conflicts with the internal liability
policy and requires legal review.

## Acceptable Data Usage Clause

Vendor may use customer data only to provide, secure, maintain, and support the
services for customer. Vendor may create aggregated usage statistics only if the
statistics do not identify customer, users, customers, or confidential business
information. Vendor will not sell customer data or use it for unrelated products.

## Negotiable Data Usage Clause

Vendor may use de-identified usage data to improve service reliability and
security. Vendor will not train artificial intelligence models on customer data
without customer approval. This clause is generally acceptable if de-identifying
controls and subprocessor disclosures are present.

## Prohibited Data Usage Clause

Vendor may use customer data, prompts, outputs, and support tickets to train,
fine-tune, evaluate, or improve machine learning and artificial intelligence
models, including models used for other customers. Vendor may share aggregated
insights with partners. This clause requires legal review.

## Acceptable Termination Clause

Either party may terminate this agreement for material breach if the breach is
not cured within 30 days after written notice. Customer may terminate hosted
services for convenience on 30 days written notice. Upon termination, vendor
will return or delete customer data within 30 days.

## Negotiable Termination Clause

Either party may terminate for uncured material breach after 45 days written
notice. Customer may terminate for convenience at the end of the then-current
subscription period. This clause is negotiable when the vendor does not process
customer data and fees are not prepaid.

## Prohibited Termination Clause

Vendor may suspend or terminate services immediately for any suspected breach,
customer must pay all fees for the remainder of the term, and vendor may retain
customer data for business purposes after termination. This clause requires
legal review.

## Acceptable Payment Terms Clause

Vendor will invoice monthly in arrears. Invoices are due Net 30 after customer
receives a valid invoice. Customer may withhold disputed amounts in good faith
while paying undisputed amounts on time.

## Negotiable Payment Terms Clause

Vendor will invoice annually in advance and invoices are due Net 45. Customer
may dispute invoices in good faith. This clause requires procurement review if
prepayment exceeds 25% of annual fees or if the vendor is not already approved.

## Prohibited Payment Terms Clause

Fees are due immediately on contract signature, are non-refundable in all cases,
and vendor may suspend services during any invoice dispute. Vendor may increase
fees at any time on notice. This clause requires procurement review and may
require legal review.
